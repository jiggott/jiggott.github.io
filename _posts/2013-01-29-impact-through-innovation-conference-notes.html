Today I attended Justgiving’s Impact through innovation conference. Topics ranged from social media to the 21st century to mobile to volunteering to in-mem giving. Here are eight things I learned:

1. Most people haven’t seen a good Vine video yet

I asked five people this question and only one replied in the affirmitive. Vine is very new so that’s not too surprising. Serendipitously, @LondonKirsty chose today to share her Storify collection of good charity Vines: I heard it through the Grapevine.

2. You can launch a campaign on Twitter before it goes out on posters, TV, etc

This is what BHF did for the #hardandfast campaign with Vinnie Jones and the results were so successful they’d do it again like that. Thanks to @DaraNasr for that.

3. JustGiving encourages a share after every action

Make a donation, set up a page, add content to your page, even edit your post-donation comment and JustGiving will ask you to share far and wide, according to @jon_bedford.

4. There’s a Facebook Page Manager app

It works on iPhone and Android and it’s more powerful than the page management tools in their regular app.

5. Lucy Buck from Child’s I (@childsi) is worth listening to

I only caught the first ten minutes of her talk but I got two gems from it:

  • Giving love is just as valuable as giving time or money
  • Storytelling 101: set up problem (beginning), offer way to help (middle), show result (end)

6. Phonecount.com is a very cool website

It’s also very useulf if you need to convince people about the value of mobile. Thanks @barryoreilly for that.

7. It’s possible to have a mobile-only user journey

This would involve an SMS or a donation SMS to start, regular outbound SMS comms and regular giving through your phone bill. Most user journeys will be mobile and something else but mobile-only is possible. Thanks @helenjpattinson for that.

8. Don’t silo your in-mem supporters

They often want to do challenges or events in memory of someone, as well as funeral collections or donations in lieu of flowers. In fact, 38% of in-mem donors skip the funeral collection / flowers thing altogether. Thanks to Katie Simmons for that.
