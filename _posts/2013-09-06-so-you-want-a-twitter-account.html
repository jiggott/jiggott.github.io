I think I hear “we want a twitter account” now more often than “we want a microsite”. It’s the new cri de coeur for teams who think they’re different from the rest of the organisation, and who feel that the existing comms aren’t reaching their audience or generating the donations, signups or registrations they require.

It’s easy to roll your eyes and think “here comes another one” when someone asks for their own Twitter account, but every now and then it’s a genuinely good idea. So how do you work out if the latest request is silly or sensible?

As of last week (when I had three separate Twitter requests from three different teams) I ask them to fill in a little form I put together. This serves four purposes:

  • It filters out the people who don’t want to invest any time in actually getting their Twitter account off the ground (they’re terrible clients anyway).
  • It gives people an idea of the effort and responsibilities involved in creating and maintaining a successful Twitter presence.
  • It gets people to think about topics they probably haven’t considered, like software, passwords and training.
  • It provides you with the information required to make a judgement about whether they require a separate Twitter account and what support they might need.
