I’ve just finished reading Leading Digital Transformation: Recommendations for Charity Chief Executives (by Katie Smith, Alex Morrison and Fay MacDonald). I’ll be passing it around others in my organisation.

There are six recommendations in the paper, some more relevant to us than others. Some of them we are already working towards, others are new  concepts we’ve not discussed before. For me, the report is confirmation that we are doing the right thing as well as some practical ideas about how we can do the right thing a bit better. It’s also that valuable weapon in your armoury for when you need to convince your bosses about something - someone else’s opinion, preferably an impartial someone.

Download a copy for yourself here:
www.cogapp.com/leading-digital-transformation-report

To give you a flavour of its content, here are some of the passages I highlighted:

Plans for digital operations that do not allow for growth rates of at least 20% per year may look like underestimates.
Digital managers in Third Sector organisations described how a lack of digital literacy across their organisations is manifesting itself in a tendency towards unrealistic expectations and a lack of prioritisation and focus.
Digital strategy is not separate from corporate strategy. It is about taking a conscious view of how online technologies can help the business to improve, whether that is reaching more people, delivering services in new ways, or raising more money.
Leaders need to equip themselves and their teams with enough knowledge to be able to make business-critical decisions about investment in digital.
